True Crime Frankenstein

A modern take on the classic Frankenstein tale, this musical takes the tale and infuses it with pop music, ensemble theater sketch comedy, and modern technology to assist a high schooler with her true crime podcast. Armed with a microphone and her own ambitions, she launches an investigative podcast to expose one Victor Frankenstein’s secrets, unraveling a tale of dark science, monsters and an alarming amount of preventable murder. The deeper she digs, the more she realizes she’s not only telling this story — she’s in it. A musical comedy about monsters and how you make them — complete with local ads!

This was a collaboration with Concord Theatricals and Penn State Centre Stage, to bring this wonderfully funny rendition of Frankenstein to life for the first time! Working alongside lighting designer William Kenyon, we had 4 short weeks to turn out the design for this musical. We quickly focused our design on the fast-paced and funny nature of this show, with lots of quick bumps and jumps through the cue stack, and interplay with the actors to elevate the jokes throughout the script.

Despite the lightning fast turnaround, we worked in tamdem to create an atmosphere that complimented the director's and writer's vision of the world to rave reviews! I was responsible for about 1/2 of the final design. The end of Act 1 and large swaths of Act 2 came from me, with my ideas and suggestions sprinkled in other places that I didn't specifically design. This show is destined for the high school market, and I'm very happy to have the opputunity to help influence it's direction in the early stages of the show's life!

Role: Associate Lighting Designer
