Little Shop of Horrors

This was a rep show that was on it's 4th and final stop for the summer. For me, this was my first large-scale outdoor show, and I used skills I previously picked up on smaller shows to some success. I had an assistent and a regular electrician working under me with various skill levels.

Being in a new space proved challenging, but with help from my assistant Louis (who helped catch many of my errors), we were able to get everything hung, curcuited and tested in record time. I also had to take care of some administrative duties, such as compliling daily schedules and dealing with outside companies to get materials needed for certain tasks. Of course, mistakes were made, and this show really pushed me hard and help me develop a lot of organizational and manigorial skills that I now use every day in my work.

While there were a couple of issues during tech, as there always is, they all got ironed out by the preview and the show opened on time to much fanfare. I cannot thank enough the people who helped and supported me throughoug this challenging process, including Josh, Mae and especially Tammy. This show would not have been possible without their wisdom and support.
