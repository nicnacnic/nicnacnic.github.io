Metamorphoses

Set in a pool of water, Metamorphoses combines primal storytelling and modern sensibility in a sensual re-envisioning of Greek myths.

The pool proved to be the biggest challenge to deal with. Not only did it take up a large portion of the production budget, there were many logistical challenges that came with having thousands of gallons of water on a stage deck. For example, we couldn't access our fixtures once the pool was filled, meaning we needed to make sure everything was hung and focused correctly before the main part of the set was installed.

Some other things that I was tasked with was the chandelier hanging from the grid. I spent a day gutting it and rewiring it to fit the designer's spec. We also had issues with our fog machine, which was too loud where it was positioned, so I had the idea of placing it in the basement with a tube and a fan to keep the noise down.

Even with the challenges, this production went very well and got praise from every angle. It went so smoothly that the lighting designer, David Heguy, even called it "the easiest tech I've ever had".
